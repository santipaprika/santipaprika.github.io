    Overview

    Hey! I am Santi Gonzalez (he/him)(also known as Santi Paprika on the internet), a 23-years-old computer scientist from Barcelona (Spain) licensed in Audiovisual Systems Engineering (Universitat Pompeu Fabra, UPF) and currently pursuing a Master’s Degree in Innovation and Research in Informatics, specialized in Computer Graphics & VR (Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya, UPC). My passions are video games, computer science, music, and series/anime. I consider myself a dynamic and open-minded learner, with a good sense of intuition.

    Computer Science

    Professionally, the role that best fits my current qualifications is graphics and game programming, open to both industry and research.

    Academic background

    I did my high school years in Girona (Spain), my hometown, and then moved to Barcelona in 2016 to course the Bachelor’s degree in Audiovisual Systems Engineering. By the end of the degree, computer graphics and games were my main fields of interest, so I decided to deepen my knowledge on them by pursuing the Master’s Degree in Innovation and Research in Informatics, specialized in Computer Graphics & VR. So far I am learning so much!
    The expected graduation date is June 2022.

    In parallel, I have completed some other online courses:

    • Unreal Engine C++ Developer: Learn C++ and Make Video Games
    • Algorithm Design and Analysis
    • CS50’s Web Programming with Python and JavaScript
    • Understanding Quantum Computers

    Professional background

    Current

    I am currently working as Graphics Research Intern at Huawei (Ireland Research Center).
    Also, I am collaborating with the Interactive Technologies Group (GTI), at UPF, exploring and implementing techniques for real-time realistic hair rendering, which relates to my master thesis.

    Previous

    [2019]
    I worked as an intern in a start-up (Plyzer). I was in the AI and Computer Vision research team.
    My task was to identify different features from images and classify them in custom categories, using afterwards this information to compute matching probabilities between some of them. Implicitly, this involved developing convolutional neural networks (CNN), and building templates with many images to tag using the custom categories.

    [2019-2020]
    For my bachelor’s degree final project I made a stance in the Full-Body Interaction Lab (FubIntLab), at UPF. I worked in a small team to develop augmented reality (AR) experiences for Barcino roman heritage site through novel AR paradigms.

    [2020]
    Development of a couple of game prototypes as freelancer, through the Fiverr platform.

    [2020-2021]
    Solo development and maintenance of an addon focused in time management and stats for the game World of Warcraft, with +1K downloads by the time being.
